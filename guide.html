Guide

Here are some small tips to getting started on your game!


Concept


What exactly is a game's concept? Well it's a lot of things. The main point a your concept is to give you a vision on what you want to accomplish. This may include the mechanics of the game, characters, artstyle, and the goal.


There's no real trick to coming up with a concept. You can take ideas from other games and media, but it all comes down to creativity. The first thing you might have your head on is the genre/artstyle. Take a minute to think, even grab a piece of paper and start writing down what comes to mind.


Some general genres include...


  • Horror
  • Action
  • Puzzle
  • Mystery
  • Platformer
  • First Person Shooter
  • Role Play Game
  • Sandbox

The list goes on. Don't be afraid to mix and match genres as well (ex. Horror and Puzzle).


Some general artstyles include...


  • Lowpoly
  • Pixel Bit
  • Doodle
  • Monochromatic
  • Flat


Choosing an Engine


Now what are you going to make your game with? If you haven't already decided, here are some well-known game engines.


  • Unity
  • Unreal Engine
  • Roblox Studio
  • GameMaker


What game engine you choose really comes down to what you have planned. For a little bit of everything, Unity is your go to.



Organize your game!


Organization is one of the most important factors when it comes to making a game. Whether it be what you plan or how you store assets, you'll need to keep it organized. Trello is a free website that allows you to make to-do list for pretty much anything. GitHub is useful for making backups and sharing files with other developers. You can also use Google Docs to write out detailed plans and lore for your game. Consider getting an external hard drive dedicated to storing your game's assets- also make sure your file hierarchy has a nice organization.
